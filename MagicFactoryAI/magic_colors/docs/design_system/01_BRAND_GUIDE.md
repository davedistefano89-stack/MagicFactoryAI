# 01 — Magic Colors · Brand Guide
**Document:** Official Brand Identity v1.0
**Audience:** Internal team, marketing, partners, localization
**Owner:** Magic Colors — Creative Direction
**Status:** Frozen baseline for Sprint 1 and beyond

---

## 1. Mission

Magic Colors exists to make **the first creative experience on a phone feel magical** for children aged 3–8. We turn a single tap into a burst of color, every stroke into a choice that matters, and every finished page into a memory a child wants to show a parent.

> **Mission statement:**
> *Give every child the feeling that their imagination is real, and every parent the confidence that screen time was worth it.*

## 2. Vision

By 2030, Magic Colors will be the **most loved coloring app in the world for children 3–8** — the one parents install first, kids return to without prompting, and schools recommend for early creativity development.

We measure this not by downloads, but by **completed pages**. A child who finishes one drawing a week, for a year, has finished fifty pages of imagination. Our north star is **one Magic Colors page per child per week, globally**.

## 3. Target Audience

### 3.1 Primary — Children (3–8 years)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Age | 3–8 (illustrative mascots reflect 4–6 range) |
| Cognitive stage | Pre-reader to early reader |
| Motor skill | Developing — large touch targets, forgiving hit areas |
| Attention span | 4–12 minutes per session is the sweet spot |
| Reading ability | Icon-first; text optional via voice cues |
| Emotional drivers | Discovery, mastery, surprise, belonging |
| Frustrations | Losing progress, ads mid-play, hard-to-press buttons |

**Design rules derived from this persona:**
- Hot zones ≥ 64 dp tall.
- Single cognitive task per screen.
- Every action has a reward cue (sound + visual).
- No text-only affordances. Always pair icon + shape + color.

### 3.2 Secondary — Parents (28–45 years)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Decision maker | Pays for premium, sets time limits |
| Anxiety | Inappropriate content, screen-time guilt, dark patterns |
| Wants | Educational value, ad-free, safe, no chat/messaging |
| Trust signals | Brand reputation, App Store age rating, parent reviews |

**Parents reach the app via:**
- App Store top-10 in "Education" and "Kids 3–5".
- Word of mouth from other parents.
- Pediatric / educator recommendations.

### 3.3 Tertiary — Educators & Therapists

Used in:
- Kindergarten art-time.
- Pediatric waiting rooms.
- Occupational therapy for fine-motor skill development.
- Speech-language gentle reinforcement.

## 4. Educational Value

Magic Colors is **entertainment first, education by design**. Every feature must answer:

> *What does a child practice when they use this?*

| Skill area | Practice surface in Magic Colors |
|---|---|
| Fine motor control | Stroke precision, tap accuracy, drag-and-drop |
| Color recognition | Color palette naming on first use, color mixing hints |
| Pattern recognition | World completion % Thumbnail reward pop-ups |
| Patience & persistence | Multi-step coloring pages, locked tools |
| Self-expression | Free drawing mode, palette size 24 colors |
| Reading readiness | Word caption near finished page ("A red apple") |
| Spatial reasoning | Layers, undo/redo, reposition stickers |
| Cause & effect | Bucket fill — every tap has a visible result |

Marketing language: *"Color, play, learn."* (Italian: *"Colora, gioca, impara."*)

## 5. Core Emotions

Every screen, button, and animation must do at least **one** of:

- **Wonder** — "Wait… what just happened?" (sparkle burst, mascot bounce)
- **Pride** — "I made that!" (gallery save, parent-share card)
- **Joy** — Laughter (chest burst, reward pop-up overshoot)
- **Belonging** — "My friend has this!" (constellation stars, world badges)
- **Safety** — "This place is gentle on me." (no dark leaps, predictable reward rhythm)

We never use: anxiety, frustration, shame, urgency, FOMO, or scarcity pressure.

## 6. Brand Personality

Magic Colors is a **kind, slightly mischievous best friend** who paints for a living.

### 6.1 The Five Personality Traits

| Trait | We are | We are NOT |
|---|---|---|
| **Cheerful** | Bright voices, upbeat music, smiling faces | Sarcastic, edgy, cool-because-ironic |
| **Patient** | Forgiving input, undo everywhere, no time pressure | Punishing, punishing-timer, "you failed" |
| **Generous** | Daily coins, surprise gifts, easy unlocks | Paywalled-progress, energy meters |
| **Curious** | Hidden easter eggs, world's-bonus rooms | Clickbait, fake-reward notifications |
| **Honest** | Real disclosure in Parents Area, no dark patterns | Loot boxes that hide odds, fake countdown timers |

### 6.2 Voice Examples

| Context | We say | We never say |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome | "Hi friend! Ready to color?" | "Hey dude, let's paint bro" |
| Empty palette | "Pick a color, any color!" | "Select from available options" |
| Reward | "Wow, you did it!" | "Congratulations, you have unlocked" |
| Locked item | "Earn it as you play!" | "Purchase premium to access" |

## 7. Brand Keywords

The seven words that must describe every creative decision:

1. **Magical** — sparkle, breathing, overshoot
2. **Soft** — rounded corners ≥16 dp, no sharp edges
3. **Colorful** — never monochrome, always rainbow-leaning
4. **Friendly** — mascot visible on first screen
5. **Curious** — small surprises in the background
6. **Safe** — predictable reward rhythm, no dead-ends
7. **Owned** — every page is saveable, exportable, shareable

## 8. Tone of Voice

| Channel | Tone | Sample |
|---|---|---|
| In-app copy | First-person, warm | *"I made this drawing just for you."* |
| Push notifications | Second-person, gentle | *"Mascot found 50 coins under the cushion!"* |
| Marketing | Confident, curious | *"What will your child paint today?"* |
| Parents Area | Honest, minimal | *"We collect X. We don't collect Y."* |
| App Store description | Energetic, factual | *"200+ coloring pages. 12 worlds. 1 magic brush."* |

### 8.1 Tone rules

- Always present tense for state (*"You're painting a unicorn"*).
- Never use "limited time", "don't miss out", "act now".
- Use sentence-case headlines, Title-Case only for buttons.
- All copy ≤ 8 words on Home. Never longer.

## 9. Brand Pillars

Three pillars anchor every feature:

- **Pillar A — Magic that earns its name.** Every art asset sparkles, breathes, or reacts. Static is forbidden in pre-school experience.
- **Pillar B — A parent can hand this to their child and walk away.** No surprise purchases, no chat, no external links in the child zone.
- **Pillar C — A child's work is forever.** Saved work persists across reinstall. Pages are exportable as PDF or printable. This is the creator economy at age 5.

## 10. Don'ts (brand red lines)

- ❌ No ads in the child zone. Ever.
- ❌ No chat, no multiplayer strangers, no user-generated content.
- ❌ No microtransactions that block content above age 6.
- ❌ No scary imagery (no realistic zombies, horror, jump-scares).
- ❌ No real-world currency symbols on coin/gem widgets (decorative only).
- ❌ No emoji as primary mascot communication (custom mascot only).
- ❌ No third-party tracking SDKs in the child zone.
